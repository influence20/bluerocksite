Frequently Asked Questions

How is cryptocurrency taxed?

In most jurisdictions, cryptocurrency is treated as property for tax purposes, which means:

  • Selling cryptocurrency for fiat currency (like USD) is a taxable event, subject to capital gains tax
  • Trading one cryptocurrency for another is also a taxable event
  • Using cryptocurrency to purchase goods or services triggers capital gains tax on any appreciation
  • Mining or staking rewards are typically taxed as ordinary income at the fair market value when received
  • Receiving cryptocurrency as payment for services is taxed as ordinary income

The tax rate depends on your holding period (short-term vs. long-term) and your income tax bracket. Our cryptocurrency tax specialists can help you navigate these complex rules and implement strategies to minimize your tax burden while maintaining full compliance.

What is tax-loss harvesting and how does it work?

Tax-loss harvesting is a strategy that involves selling investments that have declined in value to realize capital losses, which can then be used to offset capital gains and potentially reduce your tax liability. Here's how it works:

  1. Identify investments in your portfolio that have unrealized losses
  2. Sell these investments to realize the losses for tax purposes
  3. Use these realized losses to offset capital gains from other investments
  4. If losses exceed gains, you can use up to $3,000 of excess losses to offset ordinary income
  5. Any remaining losses can be carried forward to future tax years

To maintain your investment strategy, you can reinvest the proceeds in similar (but not "substantially identical") investments to avoid the wash sale rule, which would disallow the loss if you repurchase the same or substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale.

Our tax optimization service includes proactive tax-loss harvesting throughout the year, not just at year-end, to maximize the tax benefits of this strategy.

How can I minimize taxes on my investment income?

Several strategies can help minimize taxes on investment income:

  • Asset Location: Place tax-inefficient investments (like bonds or REITs) in tax-advantaged accounts and tax-efficient investments (like index ETFs) in taxable accounts
  • Tax-Efficient Funds: Use ETFs and index funds that generate minimal capital gains distributions
  • Hold for Long-Term: Maintain positions for more than one year to qualify for lower long-term capital gains rates
  • Tax-Loss Harvesting: Strategically realize losses to offset gains
  • Tax-Exempt Securities: Consider municipal bonds for tax-free income (subject to certain conditions)
  • Qualified Dividends: Focus on investments that generate qualified dividends, which are taxed at lower rates than ordinary dividends
  • Retirement Accounts: Maximize contributions to tax-advantaged accounts like 401(k)s, IRAs, and HSAs
  • Charitable Giving: Donate appreciated securities directly to charity to avoid capital gains tax

Our tax optimization service creates a personalized strategy that incorporates these and other techniques based on your specific financial situation and goals.

What are the tax implications of staking and yield farming in DeFi?

Decentralized Finance (DeFi) activities like staking and yield farming have complex tax implications that are still evolving as regulatory guidance develops. Generally:

  • Staking Rewards: Typically taxed as ordinary income based on the fair market value when received, with a cost basis established for future capital gains/losses
  • Yield Farming: Rewards are generally taxed as ordinary income when received, and subsequent transactions (like providing liquidity or swapping tokens) may trigger additional taxable events
  • Liquidity Pool Tokens: Receiving LP tokens may be considered a non-taxable event by some practitioners, while others treat it as a taxable swap; selling or redeeming LP tokens typically triggers capital gains/losses
  • Impermanent Loss: Currently no clear guidance on how to treat impermanent loss for tax purposes, though some approaches include claiming it as a capital loss

Given the complexity and evolving nature of DeFi taxation, our cryptocurrency tax specialists stay current with the latest guidance and court cases to help you navigate these waters while maintaining defensible positions on your tax returns. We implement comprehensive tracking systems to capture all DeFi transactions and their tax implications.

How do Roth conversions work and when should I consider them?

A Roth conversion involves transferring funds from a Traditional IRA or 401(k) to a Roth IRA. The converted amount is added to your taxable income in the year of conversion, but future qualified withdrawals from the Roth IRA will be completely tax-free.

Roth conversions may be beneficial in these situations:

  • You expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement
  • You have a year with unusually low income or high deductions
  • You want to reduce future Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
  • You wish to leave tax-free assets to your heirs
  • You have a long time horizon before needing the funds, allowing tax-free growth to offset the upfront tax cost
  • You have funds outside the IRA to pay the conversion tax, preserving the full converted amount for future growth

Our tax optimization service includes Roth conversion analysis that models different conversion scenarios, considering your current and projected future tax rates, cash flow needs, and estate planning goals to determine if, when, and how much to convert.
